Improvised Music Archive

With this survey (of improvising musicians of all generations, genders, and backgrounds), we aim to create an overview of influential and essential albums in improvised music.

This survey is not defined from above or imposed from the outside, but growing organically from within the scene itself, an open, ever-evolving archive.

Since improvised music is not only influenced by improvised music, we ask for:

10 albums that have significantly influenced your music over the course of your life


How the idea came about
While teaching and studying improvised music in Basel, the idea emerged to provide a kind of historical overview of important movements, bands/ensembles, musicians, and albums in improvised music.

The field of improvised music over the past 70 years has been far less thoroughly explored than, for example, electronic music, jazz, or contemporary composed music, and fortunately there is no canonical reference work

that dictates which musician or album everyone engaged with improvisation must know.
In attempting to create a timeline of important albums, it became clear how personal such a selection is.
But this is precisely one of the strengths of improvised music, one that is not officially codified and is constantly reshaped by each person who practices and listens to it.
Every musician’s voice carries its own imprint, shaped by the albums they have listened to.
We see this grassroots-like, expanding, and reciprocal network of references and influences in improvised music as a defining characteristic and an invaluable quality of this music.
